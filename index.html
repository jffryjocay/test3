ND-RVMCC: A Historical Development


Notre Dame – RVM College of Cotabato has its beginning from the Escuela Catolica founded by the Beatas (now R.V.M. Sisters) S. Isabel Cero Mojado, S. Valentina Lorenzo, S. Filomina Galos and S. Severina Santos who were sent by the Superior General of the Beatas, Reverend Efigenia Alvarez, in 1904. The first classes were held at the home of Don Antonio and Doña Eusebia Sousa, the generous benefactors of the Sisters.

In 1917, the school obtained the government recognition and was granted the authority to issue certificates to the elementary school graduates. The subject taught were Christian Doctrines, Reading and Writing, Drawing and Painting, and the Home Arts for Girls. Until 1911, all instructions were in Spanish. When English became the medium of instruction, Spanish continued to be taught as a subject in all classes of all levels.

The school underwent several name changing from Escuela Catolica to Escuela de San Antonio, Colegio de los Corazones Y Maria and finally Cotabato Parochial School which it bore until World War II stopped its operation.

After the war, the sisters organized pre-school and elementary classes under the name St. Anthony School. S. Maria Aquino secured spaces for classes in the ground floor of friends and benefactors, which the school used until the old school was rebuilt.

In 1947, Archbishop Mongeau, then the O.M.I. Father Superior, founded the Notre Dame of Cotabato a co-educational Catholic High School which in 1948 was separated into Boys Department and Girls Department. The Marist Bothers, taking over the Boys remained in the original site of the sister’s school. The RVM Sisters under the leadership of Sister M. Isabel Purification, RVM moved to the present site where the elementary school and high school for girls were fused into the Notre Dame of Cotabato – Girls Department which later became Notre Dame of Cotabato for Girls in 1979.

In the School Year 1969 – 1974, to meet the standard of its clientele, an additional building was constructed in the campus, which was named as Mother Tan Building. This served as the mini-auditorium, where school programs such as stage plays, operettes, etc. were held and at the same time served as the classrooms of the high school students.

In the School Year 1986 – 1990, as a response to the rapid increase in the school’s population, the administrators, under the able leadership of S. Ma. Remedios P. Reduta, RVM and S. Ma. Anecita C. Navaja, RVM, received blessings from its benefactors most specially from Rev. Archbishop Philip Smith, OMI, DD, hence, the Archbishop Philip Smith Building (BSB1, BSB2), were constructed and serve as the High School Department Buildings.

In 1994 – 1998, with the efforts of S. Ma. Lourdes A. Darilag, RVM, Directress, and the generosity of Archbishop Smith, OMI, DD, the school was granted another building which is now the BSB3, occupied by the Grade school pupils. This was followed by the construction of the school’s gymnasium and mini-park.

The desire to pursue quality education for its clientele was initially fulfilled when in 1987; the High School Department had its first PAASCU Accreditation while the Grade School Department had theirs in the year 2000.

In 1984, the Grade School Department started to accept boys, while in 2000, the high school Department had its first group of boys, too. After twenty – three years, that is, in school year 2002 – 2003, the school again underwent another change of name, Notre Dame – RVM School of Cotabato.

School Year 2002 – 2003 was a milestone to remember in the history of this institution. Various improvements happened this year under the leadership of S. Ma. Zosima N. Capua, RVM, the Directress. It saw the completion of the new four-storey Mother Ignacia Building which houses the Fourth Year classes, the air-conditioned library and the Speech and Internet laboratories. The Home Economics laboratory was also renovated into a mini-hotel complete with amenities. The School Chapel underwent a total face-lift. Now, it is a haven of quite and serenity. It has become a place more conducive to prayer and reflection.

With the school’s endless desire to improve and offer the community a better quality of service in the field of education, it opened its portals to college education in the school year 2003 – 2004. Two short-term courses were opened, Hotel and Restaurant management (2 years) and Caregiver Program (6months). All classes were held in the new building.

As the school readied itself for its centennial celebration in the school year 2004-2005, it anticipated fruitful year in its service towards the people of Cotabato and its neighboring towns and provinces. It is in this school year that the name was changed to Notre Dame – RVM College of Cotabato. In the same year, application for additional courses was made, to wit: a) Bachelor of Elementary Education (BEEd) and b) Bachelor of Secondary Education (BSEd) majors in: 1) English, 2) Filipino, 3) Mathematics, and 4) Physical Sciences (Physics and Chemistry).

In 2005 – 2006, S. Ma. Estelita P. Padernal, RVM, was appointed as Dean of College, and S. Ma. Jocelyn G. Gerarde, RVM as the new Directress, and the first President of the college. Both of them, obtained the permits to operate the courses. In the same year, additional TESDA courses were applied for operation in the school year 2006 – 2007, namely: a) Health Care Services (Practical Nursing), b) Computer Programming, and c) Computer Hardware Servicing (Computer Technician).

In June 2006, application for Bachelors Degreee were submitted to CHED, to wit: a) Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management (BSHRM), B) Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT), c) Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship (BSEntrep), and d) Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA) majors in: 1) Financial Management Accounting, 2) Marketing, and 3) Management. In May 2007, permits from CHED were obtained to operate in June 2007.

In school year 2008-2009, the school had a new administrator, S. Ma. Charita P. Cabunoc, RVM. She is the Second President of the College Department, Directress for Basic Education and the Principal of the Grade School Department. In this year, she led the Grade School Department in the preparation for their PAASCU revisit.

To meet the demands of quality education and global competitiveness, the ND-RVMCC acquired an institutional asset which will provide better experiential learning for its growing number of students. The asset was the former fast food joint in front of the school. This was bought by the institution last January 2008. This is the Hotel and Restaurant Management Laboratory of the college students taking up the course of Hotel and Restaurant Management.

Looking into the recreational needs of the pupils, the administration has come up with the construction of the Water Park. It has been operational since last February 2010 with the Kindergarten pupils as the first customers.

In the school year 2009-2010, S. Ma. Rosita R. Malonda, RVM was appointed as the principal in the High School Department. It was also then in the same year where an invitation from the office of the Philippine Accrediting Association of Schools, Colleges and Universities (PAASCU) that the department is entitled for the application and certification of Level III Status approved by the Federation of Accrediting Agencies of the Philippines (FAAP). Committees were formed not only for the Intensive Preparation for Level III but also for the anticipated visit in 2011. Series of seminars and workshops were attended by the faculty members in preparation for the newly introduced framework of teaching and learning which is the Understanding by Design (UbD). Facilities were improved such as the faculty room, principal’s office, and other classrooms that evidently support the quality of instruction.

In March 2010, with the efforts of the administration, the renovation of the HRM Laboratory was completed, fully equipped and furnished with stainless kitchen amenities.

Likewise in March 2010, to meet the standards of globalization, the Commission on Higher Education granted the following Bachelors Degrees: Bachelor of Science in Hotel and Restaurant Management, Bachelor of Science in Information Technology, Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship and Bachelor of Science in Business Administration. Furthermore, in April 2010, TESDA granted recognition of the Competency Based Skills as follows: Automotive, Building Wiring, Electronic, Computer Hardware Servicing, and Computer Programming.

School Year 2010-2011 witnessed the completed renovation of the Administrative Building that houses the offices of the Directress, Finance Officer, Registrar, Cashier, Assistant Custodian and the Administrators’ Conference Room. Fully furnished, the offices are now being used. Together with these renovations are three (3) high school classrooms, relocation of the grade school library at the Mother Tan Building, the Eco Park, Mini Grotto/ Ten Commandments Park, the rewiring and electrical repair of the Grade School Department which were all completed in June 2010. In July 2010, the renovation of the Business Center was completed.

The administration had also extended financial support to the faculty members and personnel in attainment of their Masters Degrees.

Being a Catholic Christian School in the midst of a highly Muslim dominated populace, it takes pride in the formation of its people and in extending its services to both Christian and Muslims alike. It still hopes to give its best as it continues to respond to the needs and realities of the present moment alongside with globalization and technology.

Vision

ND-RVMCC envisions a dynamic leaning community that empowers Ignacian Marian leaders for social transformation of the world for the common good.

Mission

ND-RVMCC commits itselt to:

1. develop Ignacian Marian leaders who are witnesses of Faith, Excellence and Service;
2. intensify instruction by pursuing innovative programs that employ relevant educational strategies for technological advancement and stewardship of creation; and
3. enhance linkages to facilitate instruction, research, and community involvement TO ENHANCE THE QUALITY OF LIFE.

Admission

To facilitate a systematic admission to ND-RVMCC, the school has established policies that need to be strictly observed. Both in-coming High School Freshmen, transferees and returning Marians are required to go through assessment, examination, interview and registration as needed.

Requirements

For In-coming High School Freshmen

1. Placement Test Results
2. Permit to enroll (PTE) from the Guidance Center
3. Form 138 (Report Card)
4. Certificate of Good Moral Character
5. Photocopy of Birth Certificate (NSO)
6. 2 pcs. 1×1 ID pictures
7. Photocopy of baptismal Certificate (for Catholics)


Transferees/ New Students

1. Placement Test Results
2. Permit to enroll (PTE) from the Guidance Center
3. Form 138 (Report Card)
4. Certificate of Good Moral Character
5. Photocopy of Birth Certificate (NSO)
6. 2 pcs. 1×1 ID pictures
7. Photocopy of baptismal Certificate (for Catholics)
8. Letter of Recommendation from the Principal of previous school


Old/ Returning Marians

1. Form 138 (Report Card)
2. 2 pcs. 1×1 ID pictures
3. Letter of Recommendation from the Principal of previous school


Admission Procedure


Assessment

Submit a photocopy of your Form 138 (Report Card) to the Registrar for evaluation purposes.

Examination

1. Pay P75.00 to the Cashier’s Office and get your Entrance Examination Permit.
2. Proceed to the Guidance and Services Center and present your Entrance Examination Permit to the Examiner.
3. You will be given a schedule and be sure to take the exam on the schedule given to you.
4. The examination covers general aptitude and essay items.
5. Get the examination result two (2) days after taking the exam.
6. Permit to Enrol (PTE) will be released to those allowed to register.
7. Those without Permit to Enrol (PTE) will proceed to the Principal’s Office for interview.

Interview

Go for interview with your parents/ guardians to:

1. Subject Coordinators in English and/ or Math (for those with academic concerns)
2. Prefect of Discipline (POD)(for those with concerns in discipline)

Important: A two week Summer Bridge Program for identified students is a requirement for official admission to the school.

Registration/ Enrollment Procedure

Step 1: Submit the following requirements to the Enrolling Officer stationed at the Mother Tan Building

1. Permit to enroll (PTE) from the Guidance Center
2. Form 138 (Report Card)
3. Certificate of Good Moral Character
4. Photocopy of Birth Certificate (NSO)
5. 2 pcs. 1×1 ID pictures
6. Photocopy of Baptismal Certificate (for Catholics)
7. Letter of Recommendation from the Principal for Transferees

Step 2: Fill out the Enrollment Form completely and correctly and have it checked by the Enrolling Officer

Step 3: Proceed to the Registrar’s Office and present accomplished form and get your priority number for the Cashier’s purpose.

Step 4: Proceed to the Cashier’s Office for payment as soon as the priority number is called.

Step 5: Proceed to the Library and get your textbooks. Present your receipt of payment to the Librarian.

Congratulations and Welcome to your new school!



Financial Policies


Payments and Refunds
Fees of all kinds and down payments for the tuition fee are paid upon registration. The balance of the tuition is paid every first week of the month in nine monthly equal installments.

Four periodical examinations are scheduled during the entire school year. You are expected to comply with the financial requirements due forn that certain examination and be able to present an admission card duly signed by the Cashier before taking the exams.

No transfer credential will be issued to the student with unpaid financial obligations.

“A student enrolled in a private school shall be considered to have registered for the entire school term. A student who transfers or otherwise withdraws, in writing, within two weeks after the beginning of classes and who has already paid the pertinent tuition fee and other school charges in full or any length longer than one month may be charged the equivalent of ten percent (10%) of the total amount due for the term if she/ he withdraws within the first week of classes regardless of whether or not she/ he has actually attended classes in the school. The student may be charged the full amount of the tuition fee and other school charges for the entire school term of he/ she withdraws any time after the second week of the classes. If the transfer or withdrawal of the student is due to justifiable reasons, she/ he may be charged the pertinent school fees only up to and including the last month of her/ his attendance, at the discretion of the school.” (Chapter VII, Articles 107, Private Education Code: November, 1975)

A student who withdraws or drops out should notify officially the Registrar and the Cashier, otherwise he/ she will be billed for the whole school year’s fees. No withdrawal is allowed starting on the month of February.
